Web Design/Project

Portfolio Website

I want to share with you the breakdown of my portfolio website which you are currently on. It was a rewarding experience to look at all the work I have done and showcase it in one place. My website visually exhibits my approach: starting with an idea and constructing it from the ground up using the skills I learned at the Art Institute.


The project is divided into four stages: sketch, low-fidelity, high-fidelity, and then code.

Site Map

Here is where I start to imagine the flow and layout of the site, making sure I have everything laid out and organized. Thus, creating the skeletons of the site

Sketch

Here is where I start to imagine the flow and layout of the site, making sure I have everything laid out and organized. Thus, creating the skeletons of the site

Low Fidelity

After working on and refining the layout, I move on to digitizing the rough sketches in Adobe Illustrator. I do this in two steps, starting with a low-fidelity version. In this step, I transfer the ideas and vision from the sketches to a digital format, focusing on speed and efficiency without worrying about too much detail. The next step will involve adding the finer details.

High Fidelity

Okay, this is the last step of the design process. Just like a lot of processes especially coding, here we are going to add some flesh to the bones; paint the walls, and put out some furniture. With this high fidelity, I am going to finalize everything. Go over the details and add some design to the pages.

Code

We have everything planned and prepared. Our final step is to bring it to life with code! Here, I am using HTML, CSS, Javascript to bring the designs to the World Wide Web. "It's Alive, It's Alive!"
